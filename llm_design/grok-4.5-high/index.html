About

An AI researcher with a career spanning system engineering at Infosys to building frontier language models at Sarvam AI. The core mission: making AI work for India's linguistic diversity, a country with 121 languages and over a billion speakers.

At ThoughtWorks, created Vakyansh, an open-source speech recognition toolkit that became part of India's national Bhashini platform. At Microsoft, worked on end-to-end speech recognition and translation for Indic languages. Now at Sarvam AI, part of the team behind Sarvam 105B and Sarvam 30B.

Author of a book on deep learning and video courses on TensorFlow. Published research at international venues and won speech recognition competitions including the IIT Madras English ASR Challenge.

Patents

Patents filed at Microsoft, focused on speech translation systems.

US Patent App. 19/038,512 · 2026

Length-Aware Speech Translation for Edge Video Dubbing

H.S. Chadha, A.S. Subramanian, S. Bansal, V. Joshi, R.R. Mehta, J. Xue

Filed at Microsoft · Published 2026

An on-device speech translation system for video dubbing that matches original speech duration. Introduces LSST and LABS for short, normal, and long variants in a single decoding pass, enabling sync without cloud dependency.

US Patent App. 18/587,660 · 2025

Translating Speech in a Gender-Aware Manner

S. Bansal, V. Joshi, R. Dsouza, R.R. Mehta, H.S. Chadha, A. Mukherjee

Filed at Microsoft · Published 2025

Gender-appropriate speech translation using speaker gender cues from audio. Uses LLMs to rectify training data, then fine-tunes the model. Shows a 70% improvement for female speakers vs baselines like Seamless M4T and Canary on MuST-SHE.
